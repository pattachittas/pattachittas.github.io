What is Patta?

Patta is an official land ownership document issued by the Tamil Nadu Government. It contains important information such as the owner's name, patta number, survey number, land area and classification. Patta is required for property registration, transfer of ownership, and applying for loans.

What is Chitta?

Chitta is a land revenue record maintained by the Village Administrative Officer (VAO). It shows whether the land is classified as wet land (Nanjai) or dry land (Punjai). Chitta was later merged with Patta and is now issued as a single Patta Chitta document.

How to Check Patta Chitta Online?

  1. Visit the official Tamil Nadu e-Services portal
  2. Select "View Patta & FMB / Chitta"
  3. Choose District, Taluk and Village
  4. Enter Survey Number or Patta Number
  5. Submit and view land record details

Details Available in Patta Chitta

  • Patta Number
  • Owner Name
  • Survey Number
  • Subdivision Number
  • Land Area
  • Land Type (Wet / Dry)
  • Village and Taluk Information

Benefits of Patta Chitta

Patta Chitta is a crucial document for proving land ownership. It is required for selling or buying land, applying for bank loans, resolving land disputes and updating land records in government offices.
